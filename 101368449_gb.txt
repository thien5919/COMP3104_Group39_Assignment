Georgebrown College or also known as GBC is located in Toronto, Canada and has three campuses which are St. James, Casa Loma, Waterfront Campus. George Brown College was founded in 1968 with just over 2,000 students. Initially, programs included construction, early childhood education, dental services, and culinary arts, alongside less relevant offerings like signwriting and watchmaking. George Brown College even had a marine engineering program.

Over time,  full-time student population has grown to around 30,000, attracting individuals from across the Greater Toronto Area, Canada, and beyond. They come here to acquire the skills and practical experience needed for success in the workforce.

Since 2004, George Brown has significantly expanded, doubling in size, establishing international partnerships, and enhancing our commitment to enriching Toronto's cultural, economic, and social landscape.
